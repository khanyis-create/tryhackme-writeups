**TryHackMe — Defensive Security Intro | Room Writeup**

Defensive security is the counterpart to offensive security. While offensive security focuses on breaking into systems, defensive security focuses on two main tasks: preventing intrusions from occurring and detecting and responding to intrusions when they happen. The teams that do this work are called **Blue Teams**.

**Areas of Defensive Security covered:**

**Security Operations Center (SOC)** - A SOC is a team of cybersecurity professionals that monitors a network and its systems around the clock to detect malicious activity. They deal with vulnerabilities, policy violations, unauthorized access, and network intrusions.

**Threat Intelligence** - The process of gathering and analyzing information about potential attackers to better prepare defenses. Understanding who your adversaries are helps predict their tactics and mitigate attacks before they happen.

**Digital Forensics and Incident Response (DFIR)** - Digital forensics involves investigating evidence of attacks by analyzing file systems, system memory, logs, and network traffic. Incident response is the structured methodology for handling a cyberattack — covering Preparation, Detection and Analysis, Containment/Eradication/Recovery, and Post-Incident Activity.

**Malware Analysis** - Studying malicious software such as viruses, trojans, and ransomware using static analysis (without running it) and dynamic analysis (running it in a controlled environment).

**Practical Exercise:**
I used a simulated SIEM dashboard to investigate security alerts as a SOC analyst. I identified a suspicious IP address, checked it against a threat intelligence database, confirmed it was malicious with 100% confidence, and escalated it to the appropriate staff member.

**Key Takeaway:** Defensive security is not just about building walls it's about constant monitoring, fast response, and understanding attacker behavior to stay one step ahead.
